Progress Overview

Class Strengths

The class demonstrates strong performance in vocabulary acquisition and basic grammar structures. Students show confidence in familiar conversation topics and are making good progress with pronunciation of common words and phrases. Most students can successfully initiate and maintain simple conversations about daily activities.

Vocabulary: Students have mastered essential everyday vocabulary and can use new words appropriately in context.
Pronunciation: Clear improvement in pronunciation of common sounds and word stress patterns.
Listening Comprehension: Good ability to understand simple conversations and follow basic instructions.

Areas for Improvement

The class needs focused attention on complex grammar structures, particularly past tense forms and conditional statements. Students struggle with fluency when discussing unfamiliar topics and need more practice with formal register and polite expressions.

Past Tense Usage: Inconsistent use of past tense forms, especially irregular verbs.
Fluency: Hesitation and long pauses when speaking about complex topics.
Formal Register: Difficulty switching between casual and formal language appropriately.

Recommendations

Increase practice with past tense scenarios, focusing on irregular verb forms through role-play activities
Implement fluency-building exercises with timed speaking activities on familiar topics
Introduce formal language patterns through business and academic conversation scenarios
Schedule individual feedback sessions to address specific pronunciation challenges
